## Typography

6–8 sizes from a consistent scale. Core scale: 12, 14, 16, 20, 24, 32, 40. Large poster/banner scale may add 56 or 72 when the headline is the main visual mass. 2–3 weights max.

Hierarchy via one property at a time: size OR weight OR color. Light bg: primary #111827, secondary #6B7280, tertiary #9CA3AF. Dark bg: #FFFFFF, #FFFFFF99, #FFFFFF66.

Fonts are loaded automatically — use any Google Fonts family (Inter, Georgia, Roboto, Playfair Display, etc.). The first render with a new font may take a moment to load.

Typography should anchor the composition. For message-led tasks, the headline is the primary visual anchor. Make it impactful but controlled: short lines, deliberate breaks, strong contrast, and no chaotic stacking.

Use a simple type system:

- Display headline: the hook; 1-2 lines when possible; usually 20-35% of visual attention on message-led graphics.
- Subheadline: clarifies the headline; shorter and quieter.
- Section heading: labels a group; smaller than display, stronger than body.
- Body: explanation; compact, readable, never decorative.
- CTA: visible action or takeaway; not louder than headline, never buried.
- Supportive label: category/date/status; small but legible.
- Micro text: use sparingly; avoid if the design is mobile/social.

Line length should stay easy to scan. Break headlines at natural phrases, not random word counts. Emphasize only 1-2 important words or phrases with weight/color/accent; do not highlight everything.

Keep line-height, tracking, text alignment, and margins between text blocks consistent. Avoid stretched or artificially wide type. Paragraphs should not run too wide; CTA should have enough contrast and spacing to scan quickly.

For Vietnamese text, use clear fonts with reliable diacritics and avoid overly condensed faces. Keep enough line height so accents do not feel cramped.

Banana Pro text safety: avoid tiny text, text on noisy photos/complex gradients, too many labels, and decorative font effects. If text accuracy matters, use fewer blocks, larger type, cleaner background, and explicit Text layers.

Readable text beats density. Do not shrink text below the composition's readable scale to fit a card. If a card becomes crowded, reduce copy, increase card size, or remove decorative/support labels. Never allow headline, body, CTA, or card labels to collide visually.
